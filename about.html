Funding Guidelines, Governance & 2030 Roadmap

Zuko Tisani Youth Leadership Foundation

Hope & Glory

Removing the financial barriers that stop capable young South Africans from starting, competing, working, and representing their country.

01 — Our Mandate

What we fund

ZTF's mandate is deliberately narrow and practical. We fund specific, once-off gateways into opportunity — not general welfare. Every grant targets a named cost, paid directly to the institution or supplier.

🎓

Registrations

University enrolment fees; entry fees for sporting events and competitions.

💼

Small business assets

Once-off capital purchases — laptops, delivery bikes, salon equipment — to start or grow a business.

👟

Sporting kit & workwear

Boots, gear, protective equipment, and uniforms for young people starting a new job.

✈

Travel to events

Flights, visas, and ground transport for young South Africans representing themselves or the country.

❤

Rare & special-case aid

Limited, case-by-case support for child-headed households and exceptional medical needs where no other funder is available.

02 — Eligibility

Who qualifies

Age & Citizenship

South African citizen, aged 15 to 35 at the date of application. A valid smart ID or green barcoded ID is required.

Preference

Preference is given to Black and otherwise disadvantaged applicants, in keeping with the Foundation's transformation and redress objectives.

Process

Applicant (or guardian, if a minor) completes the standard application, provides proof of cost, and agrees to report back — a photo, a result, a short update — once funded.

Minors

Applicants under 18 apply with a parent/guardian or school/club as co-signatory.
